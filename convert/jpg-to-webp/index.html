Why convert JPG to WebP?

WebP uses both lossy and lossless compression with better entropy coding than JPEG, so a WebP file at quality 80 usually looks identical to the JPEG but weighs noticeably less. For image-heavy pages, that compounds into real load-time savings.

JPG to WebP — FAQ

Does converting JPG to WebP lose quality?

At quality 80–90 the difference is visually imperceptible while the file is meaningfully smaller. Lower the slider for more compression or raise it for maximum fidelity.

Yes. WebP is supported by all modern browsers including Chrome, Edge, Firefox, Safari and mobile browsers.
