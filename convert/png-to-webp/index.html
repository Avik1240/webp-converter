Why convert PNG to WebP?

WebP keeps the alpha channel (transparency) that PNG is prized for, so you get the same crisp edges on logos and UI assets at a fraction of the bytes. That makes it ideal for web graphics where PNG was previously the only transparency option.

  • Transparency (alpha channel) is preserved
  • Lossless WebP averages ~26% smaller than PNG
  • Lossy mode available via the quality slider for even smaller files
  • Runs entirely in-browser — private and unlimited

PNG to WebP — FAQ

Does PNG to WebP keep transparency?

Yes. WebP fully supports an alpha channel, so transparent PNGs stay transparent after conversion.

Should I use lossless or lossy WebP for PNG?

For flat graphics and logos, a high quality setting looks lossless while staying small. For photos saved as PNG, a lower quality gives big savings.

Is WebP smaller than PNG?

Almost always. Lossless WebP is typically ~26% smaller; lossy WebP can be 60–80% smaller for complex images.

Are my PNGs uploaded anywhere?

No. Everything is processed locally in your browser.
